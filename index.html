🔤 Read Korean

Learn the 한글 alphabet. You'll be able to sound out any Korean word — even before knowing its meaning.

💡

Vowels always go beside or below a consonant. When a vowel stands alone, it's written with the silent placeholder consonant ㅇ — e.g. 아 이 우 에 오.

💡

Every syllable starts with a consonant. Consonants can also appear at the end (받침 batchim) — the "final consonant" that closes a syllable block.

Korean is written in syllable blocks, not letter by letter. Each block = one spoken syllable, always shaped as a compact square.
